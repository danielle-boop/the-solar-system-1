Our solar system

The Solar System is a vast and beautiful collection of planets, moons, and other objects that orbit around the Sun. The Sun, which is a giant ball of hot gases, sits at the center and provides the light and heat that make life on Earth possible. Around the Sun, eight major planets travel in their own paths called orbits. These planets are Mercury, Venus, Earth, Mars, Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus, and Neptune.

Each planet is unique. Mercury is the smallest and closest to the Sun, while Venus is covered with thick clouds. Earth is the only known planet that supports life, and Mars is called the Red Planet because of its color.

Venus is the second planet from the Sun. It is very hot and covered with thick clouds. People sometimes call it Earth’s “sister planet” because they are almost the same size.

Earth is the third planet and the only one known to support life. It has water, air, plants, animals, and people. It is just the right temperature for living things.

Mars is the fourth planet and is called the “Red Planet” because of its rusty color. It is cold and rocky. Scientists are studying Mars to see if it ever had life.

Jupiter is the fifth planet and the largest in the Solar System. It is a gas giant with no solid surface. It has a huge storm called the Great Red Spot and many moons.

Saturn is the sixth planet and is famous for its beautiful rings made of ice and rock. It is also a gas giant and has more than 80 moons.

Uranus is the seventh planet. It is very cold and looks blue because of the gases in its atmosphere. Uranus spins on its side, unlike all the other planets.
